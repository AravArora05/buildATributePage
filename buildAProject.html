Team History

  • 1904 - John Heisman, a pioneering figure in American football, became the head coach. Heisman's coaching tenure at Georgia Tech was highly successful, and the Heisman Trophy was later named after him.

  • 1919 - Georgia Tech, coached by Heisman, defeated Cumberland College 222-0 in the most lopsided game in the history of college football.

  • 1934 - Coach William Alexander led the team to a victory in the Cotton Bowl Classic, marking the school's emergence as a football powerhouse during his tenure.

  • 1949 - Bobby Dodd became head coach and would go on to lead the team to a 31-game unbeaten streak from 1951 to 1953.

  • 1964 - Georgia Tech left the Southeastern Conference (SEC) amid disputes over scholarship allocations and other issues, choosing to become an independent.

  • 1979 - Pepper Rodgers took over as head coach, and under his guidance, the team went on to win the ACC Championship in 1985.

  • 1994 - Georgia Tech joined the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) in football, marking a significant alignment with a major conference.

  • 2009 - Under Coach Paul Johnson, Georgia Tech won the ACC championship, but the title was later vacated due to NCAA infractions.

  • 2024 - The team, coached by Brent Key, continues to compete in the ACC, focusing on rebuilding and improving its competitive stature in the conference.
